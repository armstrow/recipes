Barbecued Pork Carolina Style

2 onions, quartered
2 tablespoons brown sugar
1 tablespoon paprika
2 teaspoons salt
½ teaspoon pepper
4-6 pound boneless pork butt or shoulder roast
¾ cup cider vinegar
4 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
1 ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
1 ½ teaspoon sugar
½ teaspoon dry mustard
½ teaspoon garlic salt
¼ teaspoon cayenne

Place onions in slow cooker. Combine brown sugar, paprika, salt, and pepper. Rub over roast. Place roast over onions. Combine vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, red pepper flakes, sugar, mustard, garlic salt, and cayenne. Stir to mix well. Drizzle about 1/3 of vinegar mixture over roast. Cover and refrigerate remaining vinegar mixture. Cover; cook on low 8 to 10 hours (or high 4 to 6 hours). Drizzle about 1/3 of reserved vinegar mixture over roast during last 30 minutes.
Remove meat and onions. Drain. Shred meat and chop onions. Serve meat and onions on buns or a sourdough roll. Drizzle remaining vinegar mixture over sandwich.

Chili Verde

3-4 pounds chicken breasts or good quality pork roast (preferably with bone)
6 Anaheim (mild) peppers
3 Jalapeno (medium) peppers
1 medium onion (optional--more pepper flavor without)
2 cloves garlic
1 tablespoon cumin
2 cups chicken stock (or 2 cubes chicken bouillon and 2 cups water)
2 tablespoons flour
Salt to taste
1-2 tablespoons sugar

Put chicken or pork in crock pot. Remove seeds from peppers (remember to wear gloves!) and add to pot. Add onion, garlic, cumin, and then pour broth over all. Cook for at least 5 hours on low (preferred) or 3 on high. Remove meat from pot, de-bone and cut (or pull) into bite-size pieces. Meanwhile, liquefy the remaining ingredients and add flour to thicken (I prefer to not bother chopping anything too much and just liquefy it all, which makes a thick green soup. If you don't have children who are squeamish about onions or peppers, you could just chop everything fine and leave it as is). Return meat to pot. Salt to taste.
Use sugar to adjust heat--adding more sugar will take the edge off any heat (really, it's amazing). You can also adjust the heat by varying the ratio of Anaheims to Jalapenos, or substituting bell or hotter peppers.

Hungarian Goulash (Cathy Stevens)

½ tablespoon butter
1 onion
2 pounds round steak, cut into 1½ inch cubes
6 tablespoons flour
1½ teaspoon paprika
1½ teaspoon salt
½ teaspoon pepper
1 can tomatoes, mashed

Sauté onion in butter until golden. Roll steak in flour and add to onion. When brown on all sides, add paprika, salt, and pepper. Add tomatoes, cover and simmer slowly until meat is tender. Pasta: Lasagna

1/2 pound ground beef
1 3/4 cup tomatoes
1/2 cup tomato sauce
1 envelope spaghetti sauce mix
1 clove garlic, minced
4 oz. lasagna noodles
6 oz. mozzarella cheese, sliced
1/2 cup cottage cheese
1/4 cup Parmesan cheese

Brown meat slowly, spoon off excess fat. Add tomatoes, tomato sauce, spaghetti sauce mix, and garlic. Cover and simmer for 25 minutes, stirring occasionally. Salt to taste.

Pasta: Macaroni and Cheese

2 cups water
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 cup elbow macaroni
1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, grated
2 tablespoons margarine
2 tablespoons heavy cream

In a 2-quart saucepan, bring salted water to a boil. Stir in macaroni, and return water to a boil. Stirring occasionally, continue boiling for four minutes. Lower the heat to medium and continue to boil the macaroni with infrequent stirring for 7 more minutes. Add the cheese, margarine, and cream, stirring until the macaroni is melted. Makes 2 one-cup servings.

Pasta: Pico de Gallo

8 ounces rotini or other short pasta
3 very ripe large tomatoes (2 ½ cups diced)
½ small sweet onion (1/3 cup diced)
2 jalapeno peppers (1/4 cup finely diced)
1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
3 garlic cloves
juice from half a lime
1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
Black pepper, to taste
3/4 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese

1.Bring 2 1/2 quarts of unsalted water to a boil in a covered 4 1/2-quart Dutch oven or soup pot. When the water reaches a rapid boil, add the pasta, and cook just until tender, according to the package directions, about 9 to 11 minutes.
2.Meanwhile, core the tomatoes but do not peel them. Dice the tomatoes, trapping the juice in a bowl or on the cutting board. Add the tomato pieces and the tomato juice to a 3-quart or larger serving bowl. Peel the onion half, and finely dice it, adding the pieces to the bowl.
3.Remove the stems, seeds and membranes from the jalapeno peppers. (Wear rubber gloves, if you have them, while working with jalapeno peppers.) Finely dice the peppers, using enough to make about 1/4 cup. Add the jalapenos to the bowl.
4.Finely chop the cilantro leaves, and add them to the bowl. Peel and finely mince the garlic, and add it to the bowl. Squeeze the juice from the lime half into the bowl. Sprinkle the salt and black pepper over the tomato mixture. Stir to mix all of the ingredients well.
5.When the pasta is done, drain it into a colander, and shake the colander to remove as much water as possible. Add the hot, drained pasta to the serving bowl with the tomato mixture. Sprinkle the cheese over the pasta, and stir to thoroughly mix all of the ingredients. Serve at once.
Pasta: Peanut Chicken Pasta

12 oz. thin spaghetti
12 oz.-1 lb. grilled or roasted chicken (cold)
1 medium red bell pepper (1 cup chopped)
4 scallions (1/2 cup chopped)
1 cup Easy Blender Peanut Sauce

Pasta: Tomato and Basil Sauce

2 pounds ripe tomatoes
½ teaspoon salt
2 cloves garlic, minced
½ cup chopped fresh basil leaves
½ cup chopped fresh parsley
2 tablespoons olive oil
¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
¼ teaspoon crushed dried red pepper (to taste)

Plunge tomatoes in boiling water for 10 seconds to peel easily. Peel and dice tomatoes (about 4 cups). Sprinkle with salt and mix well. Place salted tomatoes in a colander and allow to drain for 2 hours. Combine the garlic, basil, parsley, oil, and peppers. Add the drained tomatoes and mix well. Serve over pasta. Also good on rice, fish, or poultry.
(Note: secret to recipe is to let tomatoes drain. Another trick is to freeze ripe tomatoes, then put them in cold water to peel them.)

Swedish Meatballs (Lynette)

2 cups soft bread crumbs
¼ cup milk
½ cup chopped onion
4 tablespoons (2+2) butter
2 eggs
¾ pound ground beef
½ pound ground ham
1 cup + ¾ cup half-and-half
¼ cup finely chopped parsley
1 teaspoon salt
¼ teaspoon ginger
1 tablespoon flour
½ teaspoon concentrated meat extract
1 teaspoon Accent (msg)

Soak bread in milk for 5 minutes. Cook onion in 2 tablespoons butter until tender but not brown. Combine eggs, meats, crumbs, onion, 1 cup half-and-half, parsley, salt, and ginger. Beat vigorously until fluffy (5 minutes with electric mixer). Form in 1 inch balls. Brown and cook in 2 tablespoons butter. Remove meatballs. Stir flour into drippings, add remaining half-and-half, meat extract, and Accent. Heat and stir until gravy thickens. Return meat balls to gravy, simmer uncovered about 10 minutes. Add more milk if needed. Makes 5 dozen.
Tomatoes Provencal

2 large ripe tomatoes
2 slices white bread
2 garlic cloves, minced
1 tablespoon fresh basil
2 tablespoons fresh parsley
2 teaspoons fresh chives
salt
freshly ground pepper
6 tablespoons olive oil

Preheat oven to 375º. Core each tomato, cutting only as deep as necessary. Cut in half horizontally and gently squeeze to remove seeds and juice.
Lightly toast bread, then tear into pieces and process in food processor to make crumbs. Combine crumbs with garlic, basil, parsley, chives, salt, and pepper. Moisten with 4 tablespoons of olive oil.
Fill each tomato half with the crumb mixture, putting it into cavities and mounding on top. Place in lightly oiled shallow baking disk. Drizzle remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil over the top. Bake 20 to 30 minutes until stuffing is golden and tomatoes are juicy.
Zesty Pork Stew

1 pound pork tenderloin, cut into 1-inch cubes
2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed (2 cups)
1 medium green bell pepper, chopped (1 cup)
2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
1 cup coarsely chopped cabbage
1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning
14 1/2-ounce can chicken broth
Spray 4-quart Dutch oven with cooking spray; heat over medium-high heat. Cook pork in Dutch oven, stirring occasionally, until brown. Stir in remaining ingredients. Heat to boiling; reduce heat. Cover and simmer about 15 minutes, stirring once, until sweet potatoes are tender. Makes 4 servings.
