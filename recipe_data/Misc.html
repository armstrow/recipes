Appetizer: Frozen Grapefruit Appetizer

2 cans unsweetened grapefruit pieces
1 ½ cup sugar
1 cup water
juice of 1 lemon
Ginger ale

Mix, freeze, break pieces up, and pour ginger ale over all.
Graham Crackers

2 cups whole wheat flour
2 teaspoons baking powder
1/4 teaspoon salt
3 tablespoons brown sugar
1/2 cup margarine
2 tablespoons honey
2 tablespoons milk

Combine flour, baking powder, salt, and brown sugar. Cut in margarine. Mix in honey and milk. Roll out on greased cookie sheet until about 1/4 inch thick (it is easier to roll out on a cookie sheet without sides). Score and prick with fork. Bake at 400º for 5 minutes.
Mustard Sauce

¼ cup mustard
¼ cup vinegar
¼ cup sugar
1 egg
¼ teaspoon salt
½ cup mayonnaise

Mix mustard and vinegar, let stand overnight.
Add sugar and egg. Cook stirring constantly until thick. Remove from heat, add salt. Cool and add mayonnaise.
Dill Pickles

Wash cucumbers and cut off both ends. Pack in quart bottles along with dill weed. Add ½ teaspoon alum. Bring to boil:

1 cup vinegar
3 cups water
3 rounded tablespoons salt

Pour over cucumbers and seal.
Frozen Peach Sauce

½ box peaches
5 cups sugar
1 cup crushed pineapple (2 cups)
1 large can pineapple juice
6 ounces frozen orange juice
1 cup lemon juice (4 lemons)
6 ounces fruit fresh
Peel and dice peaches. Stir together and freeze. Partially thaw and serve on ice cream, shortcake, etc.
Frozen Jam

2 cups sugar
3 tablespoons Clear Jel
½ cup light syrup
5 cups uncrushed fruit

Was and crush fruit. Stir clear jel into sugar, add to fruit. Add corn syrup. Stir until sugar dissolves.
Note: When making raspberry jam, Mom uses 3 ½ to 4 cups of sugar to stretch the raspberries.
Pear-Apple Jam

2 cups mashed pears
1 cup peeled apples, chopped fine
¼ teaspoon cinnamon
1/3 cup lemon juice
4½ cup sugar + 1 box pectin
(or 2½ cups sugar + clear jel)
1 cup Karo

“Gloop”

2 cups Elmers glue
1½ cup water
1 cup water, divided into thirds
3 teaspoons Borax

In large bowl, mix glue and 1½ cup water. In three bowls, add 1 teaspoon Borax to each third of a cup of water. Add one at a time to glue mixture. Mix with hands.
