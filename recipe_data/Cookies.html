Chocolate Rolo

double chocolate cake mix
2 rolls of rolo candy
2 eggs
½ cup vegetable oil
powdered sugar

Mix together cake mix, eggs and oil. Place a Rolo inside a spoonful of cookie dough. Roll in powdered sugar. Bake at 350º for 10-12 minutes.
Fortune Cookies

½ cup four
¼ cup sugar
2 tablespoons corn starch
1/8 teaspoon salt
¼ cup vegetable oil
2 egg whites
3 tablespoons water

In small bowl, combine flour, sugar, cornstarch, and salt; mix well. Stir in oil and egg whites until smooth. Add water, mix well. Lightly grease a small skillet or crepe pan and place over low heat until hot. With one hand, hold pan off the heat. Pour one tablespoon of batter into the center of pan. Tilt pan in rotating motion to form a thin, even circle, about 3 ½ inches across. If batter does not spread easily, think it by adding water, ¼ teaspoon at a time. Cook over low heat till edges are brown, about 2 minutes. Turn and cook 1 minute more. Working quickly, place cookie on a float surface. Put fortune in center and fold in half, then fold again over the edge of a glass. Place in muffin tin to retain shape until cool. Repeat for each cookie, lightly greasing each time. Makes about 20 cookies.

Leslie Ott's Cookies

1 cup shortening
1 cup brown sugar
1 cup white sugar
3 eggs
1 teaspoon vanilla
1 1/2 cup flour
1 teaspoon salt
1 teaspoon soda
3 cups oatmeal (or more)
1/2 cup coconut

Cream together shortening and sugars. Add eggs and vanilla and beat well. Sift together flour, salt, and soda and add to creamed mixture. Stir in oatmeal and coconut; mix well. Bake on un-greased cookie sheet at 350º for 10 minutes or until lightly browned.

Owl (Sheryl Poulsen)

1 1/3 cup flour
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 cup brown sugar
2/3 cup shortening
1 cup crunchy peanut butter
1 egg
1 teaspoon vanilla
1 cup oats
1 oz. unsweetened chocolate
chocolate chips (for eyes)
cashews (for beaks)

Cream sugar and shortening. Stir in peanut butter, eggs, and vanilla. Add flour, baking powder, salt, and oats. Divide dough in half. Roll one half into an 8 inch long log. Add chocolate to the second half, then roll into an 8-inch square on waxed paper. Place the white log on top and wrap it in the brown, pinching the seam. Chill 1 1/2 hour.
Cut the roll into 1/4-inch slices, place 2 slices side by side and pinch ears. Use chocolate chips for eyes and cashews for beak. Bake at 350º for 12 to 15 minutes.
Rice Cereal Squares

1/4 cup butter or margarine

1/2 teaspoon vanilla

Lightly grease a 9 inch square pan. Melt butter, and marshmallows in a large pan. Add vanilla and cereal. Press mixture into pan.
Rosettes

2 cups sifted flour
¼ cup sugar
1 teaspoon salt
4 eggs
2 cups milk
4 tablespoon corn oil
2 teaspoons vanilla
2 quarts oil for frying

Sift flour, sugar, and salt. Beat other ingredients (except frying oil) and stir into flour mixture. Heat frying oil to 400º. Heat rosette iron, dip into batter, put in oil, cook until brown, then set on paper towels to drain. For eating, dip in powdered sugar while hot. For decorating, fill with icing.
Starlight Mint Surprise

3 cups flour
1 teaspoon soda
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 cup butter (or half shortening)
1 cup sugar
1/2 cup brown sugar
2 eggs
2 tablespoons water
1 teaspoon vanilla
1 package Rockwood Chocolate Mint Wafers
walnut halves (optional)

Sift together flour, soda, and salt; set aside. Cream butter, sugar, eggs, water, and vanilla. Add dry ingredients. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours. Open chocolate mint wafers; enclose each in 1 tablespoon of dough. Place on greased baking sheet about 2 inches apart. Top each with a walnut half. Bake at 375º for 10 to 12 minutes. Yields 4 1/2 dozen.
Sugar Cookies (Marian's)

1 cup sugar
3/4 cup shortening
2 eggs, beaten
1 cup sour milk
1 teaspoon vanilla
1 teaspoon soda
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 (scant) teaspoon salt
3 1/2 cup flour

Cream sugar and shortening. Add eggs, then milk and vanilla. Mix together soda, baking powder, salt, and flour, withholding 1 cup of flour. Add flour mixture, then withheld cup of flour as needed. Stir until smooth, and then chill. Roll, cut, and bake at 350º for 10 minutes.
Toffee Chip (SKOR)

2 1/3 cups flour
2/3 cup brown sugar
3/4 cup butter
1 egg, slightly beaten
2 cups (12 oz.) semi-sweet chocolate chips
1 cup chopped nuts
1 can (14 ox.) sweetened condensed milk
1 3/4 cup (10 oz) SKOR English Toffee bits

Heat over to 350º. Grease 13x9x2 inch baking pan. In large bowl, stir together flour and brown sugar. Cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add egg, mix well. Stir in 1 1/2 cups of the chocolate chips and all of the nuts. Reserve 1 1/2 cups of crumb mixture. Press remaining crumb mixture into bottom of pan. Bake 10 minutes. Pour sweetened condensed milk evenly over hot crust. Top with 1 1/2 cups toffee bits. Sprinkle reserved crumb mixture and remaining chocolate chips over the top. Bake 25 to 30 minutes until golden brown. Sprinkle with remaining toffee bits. Cool completely in pan on wire rack. Cut into bars (about 48).
Toll House Chocolate Chip

2 1/4 cup flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
1 teaspoon salt
1 cup butter, softened
3/4 cup granulated sugar
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 teaspoon vanilla
1/2 teaspoon water
2 eggs
12 oz. package (2 cups) chocolate chips
2 cups nuts

Sift together flour, soda, and salt. Set aside. Beat butter, sugar, vanilla, and water until creamy. Add eggs. Add flour mixture; mix well. Stir in chocolate chips and nuts. Drop by teaspoonfuls on greased cookie sheets. Bake at 375º for 10 to 12 minutes.
